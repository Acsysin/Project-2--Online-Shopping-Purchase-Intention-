
Online shopping sites have millions of visitors that are served daily by the booming e-commerce. A primary issue with such sites, however, is the so-called window shopping, in which they have a large number of users browsing but only a small percentage of them make any purchase. The conditions leading to the conversion of a visitor into a buyer has come to be of great research interest to the business to enhance their marketing and relationship with their customers.
Developments in data analytics and machine learning have enabled the possibility to analyze user browsing patterns and predict patterns that reflect a buying intention. Predictive models may be used to determine the probability of a purchase within a user session based by scrutinizing variables including browsing period, page, type of device and nature of the user session. This allows companies to make decisions that are more informed in terms of targeted marketing, custom-created user experiences, and upgraded websites.
The given project is devoted to creating a machine learning model that will predict the presence of a purchase at the end of a browsing session as a visitor to a webpage. the analysis of behavioral and session-based characteristics is introduced to group sessions into purchase and non-purchase. The results would help explain consumer behavior and enable companies to enhance the rate of conversion and the entire online shopping experience.
